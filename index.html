About Me


I am an entry-level web developer and a life-long puzzle solver. The latter, I believe, is the more important of the two, and is related to the former.

Throughout my life I have enjoyed solving puzzles. Sometimes this is literal; one of my most common uses of our first computer when I was a child was to look up logic deduction challenges to solve for fun. I was a strange child - that has, perhaps, not changed much now.

The puzzles, however, have become more varied since then, and often more loosely defined. For instance, my favorite part of multiple of my jobs was when I assumed the role of de-facto tech support, given that I was the only one present with the aptitude and confidence to dive in when things broke. One example is when everyone at one building I worked at who knew how to use the camera system left at the same time. No one left working there knew how to operate them, and though I was not stationed at that location full-time I was the one who sat down to puzzle it out. They ended up with a guide as to how to manage the monitoring and access control of the building again; I left having had a unusually fun day at work.

In the web development field, I am still in the process of completing the University of Washington Full-Stack Coding Bootcamp. My technology experience thus far includes HTML, CSS, Javascript, Jquery, Jquery, API usage, JSON, and Node. I will be continuing to develop back-end experience through the course, and am seeking to translate it to full-time employment in Seattle.
